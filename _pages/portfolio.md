## 2019 - Robot swarm communication capability, during visiting research Japan
Swarm robotics is a fascinating topic both in a terrestrial as in a space context. For the Space Robotics Lab, it is the aim to transform the Clover robotic platform into a swarm, thereby creating a swarm platform. This swarm platform could then be used to test and prove swarm technologies, for both terrestrial as space applications. Developing communication capability for the Clover, is a vital component of this pathway. My research is focused on developing and implementing a communication infrastructure for the Clover, whereby it can autonomously communicate with the other rovers in the swarm in a decentralised manner. Multiple methods are tested and evaluated on their merits and drawbacks.

## 2019 - Micro-satellite solar panel stowing and deployment mechanism, for course project
With two other master students, a new design of stowing, deployment and locking mechanism for solar panels on a small, 6U satellite was developed. Various ingenious design were considered, including origami folding and velcro, and in the end a bar locking mechanism was developed, proposed and 3D printed at the TU Delft facilities. A simple and reliable thermal knife mechanism was developed for the deployment of the panels, using nylon cords.

## 2016 - Communicational problems in Aviation Operation (Bachelor Thesis)
At the [Human Factors and Safety research group](https://www.amsterdamuas.com/aviation/subsites/en/aviation/research-programmes/human-factors-and-safety/human-factors-and-safety.html){:target="_blank"} in Amsterdam, which is part of the Aviation Academy, I developed a tool to analyse accident and incident reports. From a system point of view, the tool would help identify contributing factors of accidents/incidents. The aim of this research was to focus on the kind of interactions where problems would arise, to gain insight in how the form of communication may not be fitting to the information transfer. In addition to problems in human to human interactions, a multitude of problems in human-machine interactions where found. The general recommendation that is made is to focus more on the human aspect, and interpretation, within the aviation operation system; human-centered design.

[Communicational problems in Aviation Operation on ResearchGate](https://www.researchgate.net/publication/339018144_Communicational_Problems_in_Aviation_Operation_Bachelor_Thesis){:target="_blank"}

## 2015 - Maintenance capability development (Bachelor Internship)
At KLM Engineering and Maintenance, during my Bachelor internship, I identified 80+ unprofitable capabilities, saving costs on maintenance and space. The Avionics Shop, which maintains the avionic components for KLM, its partners and external customers, has a large amount of components that it can maintain, repair and test. For some old components, there was still capability to maintain and repair (i.e. testing facilities and other equipment), although these rarely came into the shop. I helped identify these particular components, and in particular their respective equipment needed for maintenance, and decommissioned these capabilities so that the shop had more room for new capabilities. In addition to this I developed four capabilities for some components of the newly introduced Embraer fleet at KLM Cityhopper at the time.
